Comprehensive Speech & Occupational Therapy Evaluations

Evaluations include standardized assessments, clinical observations, and caregiver input to identify each child’s strengths and areas of need. Results are used to develop individualized treatment plans tailored to the child’s developmental profile and school setting.

Speech Therapy Services

Our pediatric speech therapy services address underlying speech and language deficits using evidence-based intervention strategies tailored to each child’s developmental level and learning environment.

Speech therapy is provided one-on-one by a licensed speech-language pathologist on-site at the child’s school or daycare.

Speech-language therapy supports children with:

  • Speech sound disorders (articulation and phonological delays)
  • Expressive language disorder
  • Receptive language disorder
  • Mixed receptive-expressive language disorder
  • Social communication and pragmatic language disorders
  • Fluency disorders (stuttering)
  • Voice disorders

Occupational Therapy Services

Our pediatric occupational therapy services focus on supporting functional participation through evidence-based intervention strategies tailored to each child’s developmental level and daily routines.

Occupational therapy is provided one-on-one by a licensed occupational therapist on-site at the child’s school or daycare.

Occupational therapy supports children with:

  • Fine motor delays
  • Visual-motor integration difficulties
  • Sensory processing differences
  • Delays in self-care and daily living skills
  • Challenges with attention, regulation, and classroom participation

Our Clinical Approach

Star Therapy Solutions uses evidence-based practices guided by ongoing data collection, clinical expertise, and family collaboration. Therapy goals are functional, measurable, and individualized to support meaningful participation across school routines, home activities, and social environments.

Intervention strategies are continuously adjusted based on each child’s response to treatment to ensure progress remains appropriate, measurable, and developmentally relevant.

What to Expect

  1. Initial contact and developmental screening
  2. Comprehensive evaluation if recommended
  3. Individualized treatment plan based on assessment results
  4. One-on-one therapy sessions at the child’s school or daycare
  5. Ongoing progress monitoring and caregiver communication

Families are kept informed throughout the therapy process and provided with practical strategies to support carryover beyond sessions.
